What I Did

  • Created the T-SQL procedures and reporting tables in the analytics engineering layer, described here
  • Designed the Power BI semantic model for simplicity of reporting
  • Designed and built a Power BI report which allowed easy understanding of a complex relationship between data points for operational teams
  • Built several DAX measures for volumes, percentages and chart labels
  • Provided user guidance, operational definitions and calculation logic

Context

A contact centre triaging hundreds of complex cases per day comparing the initial case information with the treatment the case received across multiple teams and the ultimate outcome. The organisation wanted a better understanding of which cases flowed to which teams and whether the initial classification impacted the choice of teams or the outcome.

The project required an extensive reconstruction of queue flows from low-level case management system audit events including multiple complex scenarios, the details of which are described here.

Technologies used were T-SQL procedures creating and updating new reporting tables, Power BI for the semantic model, DAX measures and reporting canvas.

Data Model

Source data came from the two new reporting tables created at the analytics engineering layer. One table contained details of each time a case entered a queue, its status and classification at the time and other associated metadata. The second table provided all activities and outcomes that occurred within the case and the queue the case was in at the time.

A SQL view provided this data consolidated into one row per case capturing the relevant information on initial classification, team reviewing and outcome classification. This allowed the Power BI model to display comparisons between starting and ending details.

Report Design

The relatively simple semantic model was supported with lookup reference tables and relationships to aid user understanding. Reports were designed and built with operational teams in mind and used a format that enabled a correlation between two data points to be easily visualised. The stacked bar chart format with curated colouring and clear labelling enabled an easy-to-understand visual of case volumes in each comparative type. Separate pages were created to isolate each relationship for clarity.

DAX measures created included incident volume calculations, proportional flow percentages, filter-independent totals, formatted chart labels and distinct outcome levels. Drill-through pages enabled a deeper understanding of the cases involved and allowed the participants reviewing the relationships to dig deeper into the cases for further information. Additional helper pages clarified the approach and the relationships, the metrics and the classifications, along with navigation support and version control.

Outcome

The report enabled the leadership team to move away from spreadsheet and data-dump analysis of cases in all-day sessions, to a visual analysis of key case movements and outcomes that allowed a confirmatory review of the affected cases to confirm the findings.

This saved hours of time and allowed more frequent reviews of case flow, ultimately improving performance.
